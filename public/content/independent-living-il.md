# Independent Living (IL)

Good to know: Models of Disability/Impairment vs. Disability

Check out next: Ableism/The Social Environment/Universal Design

## Explanation

IL is movement closely aligned with the social model, IL states that regarding daily life : 

Disabled people should be granted with equal independence as able bodied people

## Clarification

Under the medical model: 

<aside>
🏥

Independence is living alone without any support, assistance or help. 

</aside>

Under the social model: 

<aside>
📢

Independence means having opportunities, rights and control over life

</aside>

Because everyone is interdependent upon external services, people, tools and conditions: 

- Able bodied people’s interdependence are hidden by an environment that treats it as the norm
- Disabled people’s interdependence are magnified by an environment that treats it as a flaw

| Disabled interdependence  | Able-bodied interdependence |
| --- | --- |
|   • Shoes |   • Wheelchairs |
|   • Stairs |   • Ramps |
|   • Doorknobs |   • Automatic doors |
|   • Spoken language |   • Sign language |
|   • Emotional venting |   • Emotional grounding |
|   • Package delivery  |   • Home care |
|   • Typical transit |   • Accessible transit |

 IL seeks to provide disabled people with the right to decision how interdependence is structured

## Application

**Case: Alex is a disabled student with mobility impairments**

<aside>
⏰

Without IL: 

- Alex is forced to eat, sleep and learn according to the availability of the volunteers that support him
</aside>

<aside>
📌

With IL: 

- Alex receives funding directly to hire, train, and manage their own Personal Care Assistants, allowing him to set a daily schedule
</aside>
