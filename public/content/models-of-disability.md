# Models of Disability

## Explanation

A “Model” is an particular angle that we use to understand disability

## Clarification

There are three dominant models of disability in society:

1. Medical model
    1. **Disability** is a biological problem/deficit/pathology that needs to be fixed
    2. **Disabled people** are broken patients to be diagnosed, managed and treated
    3. **Society** provides means that help disabled people become normal
2. Charity model
    1. **Disability** is a personal tragedy/misfortune/weakness that should be pitied
    2. **Disabled people** are helpless victims to be supported, cared for and saved
    3. **Society** provide means that help disabled people survive through goodwill
3. Social model 
    1. **Disability** is a difference that is rejected by the norms in society
    2. **Disabled people** are disadvantaged by barriers in the environment
    3. **Society** should be ****changed so that disabled people could participate equally

---

**The medical/charity model believes:**

The individual is the problem, they need to change for society

**The social model believes:**

Society is the problem, it needs to change for the individual

---

**The medical/charity models are criticized due to multiple shortcomings**

1. They strip disabled people of their rights, voice and autonomy by placing authority in non disabled professional/experts that do not understand the realities of being disabled
2. They undermine the dignity of disabled people by framing them as broken/pathetic, portraying disability as inherently bad when it’s another state of being
3. They assume that abled bodied people are fully independent when we are all interdependent on other and society to succeed
4. They mask existing injustices in society while placing pressure on disabled people to meet unfair standards

---

<aside>
📢

The social model is an attempt to resolve the issues above to create an alternative future of inclusion, accessibility and equality where differences are respected as a natural part of natural human diversity

</aside>

## Application

**Issue: A disabled person cannot climb flights of stairs to access his classroom**

| **Medical Model** | **Charity Model** | **Social Model** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Provide stair climbing wheelchairs | Provide volunteers to carry the student  | Install an elevator/ramp |
