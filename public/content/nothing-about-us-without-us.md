# Nothing About Us Without Us

Good to know: Models of Disability

Check out next: Independent Living (IL)

## Explanation

- “Nothing About Us Without Us” is the rallying call of the disability rights movements.
- The principle implies that when it comes to decision that affects disabled people:
    - Those with lived experience should always hold highest authority
    - Meaningful participation of the group’s members must be present

## Clarification

Under the medical/charity model:

<aside>
🏢

- Able bodied “experts”, “professionals” or “leaders” apply their detached perspectives to make decisions that affect disabled people
</aside>

Under the social model: 

<aside>
☝

- Disabled people directly apply their lived experience to guide decisions to be truly aligned with the interests of the community
</aside>

Because those disconnected with the realities of disability cannot understand the needs, wants and difficulties of disabled people

## Application

**Case: A large donation is made to a NGO with a mission to support disabled people**  

<aside>
⛔

Without “Nothing About Us Without Us”

- Able bodied board members use the money to fund “awareness campaigns” that treat disabled people as objects of pity, further exacerbating disability in society by deepening existing biases
</aside>

<aside>
✅

With “Nothing About Us Without Us”

- Disabled people partner with educators, creators and leaders to design social model informed efforts that focus on resolving real life difficulties, reducing disability through dismantling barriers
</aside>
