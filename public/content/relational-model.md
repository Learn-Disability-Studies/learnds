# Relational Model

Good to know: Models of Disability/Impairment vs. Disability

Check out next: Universal Design/Nothing About Us Without Us

## Explanation

- The relational model of disability states that disability is a state of disadvantage created by the interaction between bodily impairments and environmental barriers

## Clarification

Under the medical model

<aside>
🏥

Impairments creates disabilities

</aside>

Under the social model

<aside>
🚧

Environments create disabilities

</aside>

Under the relational model

<aside>
⚖️

Impairments+environments=disability

</aside>

The central crux is

<aside>
📌

Medical care is a choice, access is a unconditional right 

</aside>

## Application

**Emily experiences chronic pain, under the relational model:**

She can 

<aside>
💊

Chose medical interventions to reduce the real pain, discomfort and inconveniences

</aside>

Society must

<aside>
🔎

Remove barriers in to ensure she can participate regardless of medical intervention

</aside>

By changing the dynamic either direction, the state of disability is reduced

### “Fundamental Principles of Disability”-UPIAS

Foundational document that reveals the early social-relational understanding on disability that was eventually reduced. Focus on the UPIAS commentary for directly relevant information.  

> Good for: Historical Perspective, SMD basics, Theoretical Analysis

[disability-studies.leeds.ac.uk](https://disability-studies.leeds.ac.uk/wp-content/uploads/sites/40/library/UPIAS-fundamental-principles.pdf)

</aside>

### “**Rescuing a social relational understanding**”-Carol Thomas

Modern reestablishment of the social-relational model that highlights the need of revisiting ideological roots of disability studies to avoid over-simplification in modern pure social model understandings

> Good for: Concept Grasp, Advanced Reading, Theoretical Analysis
> 

[Rescuing a social relational understanding of disability | Scandinavian Journal of Disability Research](https://sjdr.se/articles/10.1080/15017410409512637)

</aside>

<aside>
⁉️

### “Disability Rights and Wrongs Revisited”-Tom Shakesphere

Widely regarded as a bridge towards social-relational understandings in disability studies. Established disability as an emergent property from the interaction of factors through a critique of pure medical/social models. 

> Good for: Historical Perspective, Advanced Reading, Critical Analysis
> 

[Disability Rights and Wrongs Revisited](https://www.routledge.com/Disability-Rights-and-Wrongs-Revisited/Shakespeare/p/book/9780415527613)
