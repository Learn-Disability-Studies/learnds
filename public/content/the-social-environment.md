# The Social Environment

Good to know: Models of Disability/Impairment vs. Disability

Check out next: Universal Design/Curb Cut Effect

## Explanation

- The barriers in society that the SMD aims to resolve are grounded in the environment
- The environment represents a collection of factors that could enhance/reduce disability

---

The environment is composed by three major pillars:

| **Attitudes** | **Infrastructure** | **Institutions** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What people think  | Built physical structures | Organizational structures |
| • Norms<br>• Assumptions<br>• Narratives<br>• Expectations<br>• Biases<br>• Prejudices | • Ramps<br>• Subtitles<br>• Elevators<br>• Doorways<br>• Signage<br>• Interfaces | • Laws<br>• Policies<br>• Standards<br>• Planning<br>• Protocols<br>• Systems |

## Clarification

Under the medical/charity model

- The environment is naturally present, therefore static, immutable and justified
- We should change the individual to match the environment

Under the social model

- The environment is socially constructed, therefore dynamic, variable and questionable
- The environment is built by us, therefore it can be rebuilt by us

## Application

**Issue: Disabled people struggle to access public spaces because only stairs are present**

| **Attitudes** | **Infrastructure** | **Institutions** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shift public mindset towards SMD | Build ramps/ install elevators  | Enforce accessibility mandates |
