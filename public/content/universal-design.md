# Universal Design

Good to know: Models of Disability

Check out next: Curb Cut Effect/Temporarily Able Bodied (TAB)/Nothing About Us Without Us

## Explanation

- Universal design is the practice of designing environments/products to be easy to use for everyone without needing additional adaptation measures
- Universal design serves as a proactive solution to barriers in the environment by treating accessibility as a prerequisite instead of an afterthought

## Clarification

Under the medical model: 

<aside>
⬇️

The environment is built only for the able bodied according to narrow assumptions on the “normal user”

</aside>

<aside>
⏹️

People with impairments are expected/forced to adapt to the unaddressed barriers creating disability

</aside>

Under the social model: 

<aside>
⬇️

The environment is designed with participation of people with impairments in mind, considering the different needs of that their diversities require

</aside>

<aside>
⏹️

People with impairments avoid becoming disabled as barriers in the environment are eliminated before they become reality

</aside>

## Application

**Case: Designing a buss stop platform** 

<aside>
⛔

Without Universal Design

- The platform requires passengers to climb a steep step to board the buss, making boarding difficult for wheelchair users
</aside>

<aside>
✅

With Universal Design 

- The platform is elevated to the same height as the bus floor, allowing wheelchair users to smoothly roll onto the vehicle
</aside>
